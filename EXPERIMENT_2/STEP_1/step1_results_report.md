# Experiment 2 — Step 1: Genome-Wide Frequency Diagnostics & Screening Matrix

## Objective & Study Track
This directory contains the foundational analytics for **Experiment 2: The Genome-Wide Discovery Screen**. 

Unlike the preliminary candidate-gene panel evaluated in Experiment 1, this independent arm of the project expands our clinical cohort of 253 unique Glioblastoma (GBM) patients to cross-reference their survival profiles against a dynamically selected pool of the top 100 most frequently mutated genes. The downstream goal is to establish an automated, high-density discovery track to feed our non-linear survival machine learning models.

## Gene Selection Methodology & Screening Logic

To avoid manual bias, the genes evaluated in Experiment 2 were selected dynamically based on empirical mutation density:
1. **Purity Filter:** Somatic raw data was parsed to eliminate synonymous variants.
2. **Patient-Level Penetrance Ranking:** Genes were ranked based on mutation frequency calculated **across unique patients**, rather than raw sample occurrence. This means a gene's status is binary per row ($1$ = mutated, $0$ = wildtype), avoiding inflation from multi-sample sequencing on single individuals.
3. **Top 100 Ingestion:** The 100 genes exhibiting the absolute highest penetrance rates were extracted to construct our high-dimensional master processing matrix.

## Codebase Artifacts & Output Specifications

Every script execution in Step 1 outputs structured files into the project environment to feed subsequent analytical steps:
1. **`top_100_genes_list.csv`**: A 2-column manifest containing the complete set of HGNC gene symbols mapped alongside their patient-level calculated penetrances (`Frequency`). This file acts as the automated loop-driver for Steps 2 through 7.
2. **`gbm_merged_data.csv`**: The expanded analytical discovery matrix. It features 253 patient rows and spans 105 total active structural columns: our core baseline parameters (`PATIENT_ID`, `AGE`, `SEX`, `OS_MONTHS`, `OS_STATUS`) plus 100 individual binary gene feature vectors.
